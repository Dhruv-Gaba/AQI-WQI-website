AIR QUALITY

Air quality in Kerala which is a southwestern state of India, is relatively better compared to many other Indian states. Kerala has a unique geographical and climatic characteristics which contribute to its better air quality.

Air quality is a measure of the composition and concentration of various substances present in the Earth's atmosphere, such as gases, particulate matter, and other airborne pollutants. It reflects the state of the air in a particular location at a specific time.

Clean and healthy air is essential for human well-being, as it is the air we breathe. Poor air quality can have detrimental effects on human life.

WATER QUALITY

Water quality in Kerala, which is a southwestern state of India is a critical aspect of environmental and public health management. Kerala's unique geographical features, abundant water resources, and various anthropogenic activities influence the quality of its water.

Water quality refers to the physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of water that determine its suitability for various uses, including drinking, recreation, agriculture, and sustaining aquatic ecosystems. It is a fundamental aspect of environmental science and public health, as the quality of water can have profound impacts on human well-being and the natural environment.

Access to clean and safe drinking water is essential for preventing waterborne diseases and ensuring the well-being of communities.Aquatic ecosystems, including rivers, lakes, and oceans, depend on clean water to support diverse species and maintain ecological balance.
